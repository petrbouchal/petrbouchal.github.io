  1. There should be time devoted to our interests SPECIFICALLY. We shouldn’t only be boxed by workshops. Participant-made workshops achieve this slightly, but for the less confident individuals, maybe there should simply be a time when they can get together and do what they enjoy (not necessarily learn something). The already active version of this is Poetry Reading (which could only be run by participants). More options are for example activities in the wilderness, board-games, etc. These may appear as things one could do in his/her free time, but since A) there is no free time, and B) many are too shy or don’t yet know each other so they don’t have the opportunity to spontaneously decide to do something together. This would nudge people to do what they enjoy.
